Sports funding for Ohio MAC Schools

With rising tuition costs and students spending more time in school to complete their degrees, college has become a costly choice. One of those factors is funding to athletic programs.

Colleges, on average, spend about seven times more on athletes than on students, and this results in more than 80 percent of colleges lose money on college sports, according to a report from New York Times Contributor Jeff Benedict and Lead Correspondent for 60 Minutes Armen Keteyian.

This application contains revenue and expenses for all the MAC schools in Ohio, as well as Ohio State in order to show how a school who does have a profitable atheltic program spends and receives their funds.
